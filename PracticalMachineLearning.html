Background

Using devices such as Jawbone Up, Nike FuelBand, and Fitbit it is now possible to collect a large amount of data about personal activity relatively inexpensively. These type of devices are part of the quantified self movement  a group of enthusiasts who take measurements about themselves regularly to improve their health, to find patterns in their behavior, or because they are tech geeks. One thing that people regularly do is quantify how much of a particular activity they do, but they rarely quantify how well they do it. In this project, your goal will be to use data from accelerometers on the belt, forearm, arm, and dumbell of 6 participants. They were asked to perform barbell lifts correctly and incorrectly in 5 different ways. More information is available from the website here: (see the section on the Weight Lifting Exercise Dataset).

Model building

On clean data we need to divide the train data set into training and cross-validation datasets. Iv decided to take a 80/20 split.

# split the train data into 2 sets - training and cross validation
inTrain <- createDataPartition(y = trainclean$classe, p = 0.8, list = FALSE)
training <- trainclean[inTrain, ]
cval <- trainclean[-inTrain, ]

We can take a look at the correlation matrix of all the predictors.

# calc the correlation matrix
cormatrix <- cor(training[, -length(training)])
corrplot(cormatrix, order = "FPC", method = "circle", type = "lower", tl.cex = 0.7,  tl.col = rgb(0, 0, 0))

We are going to deploy a radnom forest model to predict classe using all other feautres as predictors. We will use crossvalidation to build up a confusion matrix and check how the model is performing.

# fit a radnom forest model 
modelFit <- randomForest(classe ~ ., data = training)
# crossvalidate the model
predictCVal <- predict(modelFit, cval)
confusionMatrix(cval$classe, predictCVal)
